Executive Summary

StockSight is a personal automated investment research pipeline built to identify mean reversion opportunities triggered by significant single-stock price drops. The system monitors financial email alerts around the clock, extracts candidate stocks using AI, and routes them through a structured multi-layer evaluation framework before surfacing a scored verdict to the human investor.

The system does not trade autonomously. It produces structured, scored, documented signals. All capital allocation decisions are made by the human operator after reviewing system output. The system's goal is to eliminate manual monitoring overhead and impose discipline on a signal-extraction process that is otherwise noisy, time-consuming, and prone to cognitive bias.

Problem & Baseline

Mean reversion trading on large single-stock price drops is a well-documented edge in academic literature (Savor 2012; Dunis, Laws & Rudy 2011). The practical challenge is not the strategy — it is the pipeline required to act on it reliably:

Before StockSight

  • Manual inbox scanning across multiple email alert sources
  • No consistent criteria for what counted as a valid signal
  • Ad hoc evaluation: easy to talk yourself into or out of trades
  • No structured record of why a position was entered or closed
  • No feedback loop: impossible to calibrate over time
  • Time cost: 30–60 min/day of unfocused monitoring

Core pain points

  • Signal noise — most alerts are irrelevant (fraud, sector moves, stale news)
  • Cognitive load — evaluating 5 potential stocks manually is exhausting
  • Inconsistency — different criteria applied each day
  • No structured documentation — no way to review past decisions
  • No calibration — no ability to improve the process over time
C

Objectives & Non-Goals

Objectives

The system is designed to:

  • ✦ Automatically detect qualifying stock drop events from email alert streams
  • ✦ Filter noise and reject structurally invalid signals before human review
  • ✦ Apply consistent, documented, rule-based evaluation to every candidate
  • ✦ Produce a structured, scored, auditable verdict for each case
  • ✦ Track all cases in a structured Tracker with outcome recording
  • ✦ Accumulate enough closed cases to calibrate and improve the model
Explicit non-goals. StockSight does not make autonomous investment decisions. It does not execute trades. It does not predict price targets with certainty. It does not replace fundamental analysis for long-term positions. All scoring is advisory — final decisions remain with the human operator.

This boundary is architecturally enforced: the system has no write access to any brokerage account, and every evaluation explicitly ends with a human review step before any real capital is committed.

System Design & Workflow

The system runs in two parallel pipelines: an automated intake (PS Scan) and a structured evaluation (Gate+SS), connected via a staging area in Google Sheets.

Pipeline overview

  • Gmail inbox Financial alert emails from Google Alerts, ADVFN, and Benzinga are automatically labeled StockSight_Pending by Gmail filters. No manual inbox scanning required.
  • PS Scan (6am + 6pm) Apps Script reads labeled threads (up to 5 per run), extracts body text, and sends it to Gemini AI with a structured extraction prompt. Gemini returns a JSON payload of candidate stocks and rejects.
  • Tracker intake Valid candidates are written to the Tracker sheet (cols A–G) with status NEW. Rejects are logged to the Rejected Cases tab with reason. Duplicate detection prevents re-processing active positions.
  • Gate+SS evaluation Claude AI evaluates each NEW row via an 11-layer structured evaluation: hard gate, live data fetch from Stock Events, sector context, gate criteria, management credibility, scoring, catalyst analysis, bear case, and trade type classification.
  • Updates tab sync Evaluation output (TSV format) is pasted into the Updates tab. The operator checks the A1 checkbox or waits for the 11pm safety-net trigger. Apps Script writes the evaluation columns (H–S) into the correct Tracker row.
  • Monitoring & alerts The 7am daily check trigger monitors ACTIVE positions against stop loss, target, checkpoint dates, and time exits. ARRAYFORMULA columns auto-calculate stop loss, target, return %, and P&L from locked entry prices.
  • Human review At each stage (evaluation, entry decision, checkpoint, closure), the human operator reviews system output before taking action. The system surfaces structure; the human makes decisions.

Rules, Constraints & Guardrails

The system is structured around explicit, documented rules at every layer. These are not soft guidelines — they are hard filters built into the prompt instructions and Apps Script logic.

PS intake filters (Gemini)

Include only if all present

  • Named ticker or recognisable company
  • Specific event type confirmed
  • Numeric drop ≥8% (US) / ≥6% (EU) stated explicitly

Immediate reject categories

  • Securities fraud / accounting restatement
  • Private companies (not listed)
  • Sector-wide or index-level drops
  • Stale events (>5 trading days old)
  • Drop % not stated numerically

Gate hard blockers (Claude)

These cause an immediate REJECT regardless of drop magnitude:

Fraud / bankruptcy risk Trading halt / suspension Drop < 8% (US) Event > 5 trading days old Unknown event category Required field missing

Experiment phase constraint

For the first 60–80 closed cases, Gate verdicts are informational only — every case proceeds to full evaluation regardless of Gate outcome. This prevents premature hard-blocking before the system has enough data to validate each criterion. Calibration happens at case 60.

Role of AI vs Deterministic Logic

A deliberate design decision separates what AI does from what rules do. AI is never trusted for binary gate decisions — it is used for extraction, synthesis, and structured judgment within bounded frameworks.

TaskWho does itWhy
Email text → structured JSON Gemini AI (PS Scan) Unstructured extraction from noisy email bodies; AI handles formatting variance
Ticker deduplication Apps Script (deterministic) Rule-based — active tickers always excluded, no ambiguity tolerated
Event cause verification Claude AI (Gate) Requires judgment: is this a real event? Does it match known categories?
Live data fetch (price, volume, 52W) Claude AI (Layer 2) Structured extraction from Stock Events page; deterministic from fixed source
Scoring (Clarity, Bounded, Business, Reaction) Claude AI (Layer 6) Multi-dimensional judgment within a fixed rubric; AI applies defined criteria consistently
Stop loss / target calculations ARRAYFORMULA (Google Sheets) Pure math; no AI needed; locked to actual entry price
Checkpoint scheduling Apps Script (deterministic) Date arithmetic; no judgment required
Entry decision Human Capital at risk; human retains full authority
G

Evaluation Logic — 11-Layer Framework

Every candidate that passes PS intake is evaluated through a fixed 11-layer sequence. Layers run in strict order; later layers do not run if a hard gate triggers at Layer 1.

  • Layer 1
    Hard Gate     Rejects fraud, bankruptcy risk, halted stocks, drops under threshold, stale events, and missing required fields. Non-negotiable — no AI judgment involved at this stage.
  • Layer 2
    Data Fetch     Fetches live data from Stock Events: current price, 52W high/low, volume vs average, upcoming events within 7 days, EPS vs estimate, revenue trend, analyst ratings, profit margin, P/E.
  • Layer 3
    Sector Context     Web search for sector performance on event day. If sector down ≥3%, the drop is partially sector-driven — Clarity score reduced by 1, flagged as SECTOR CONTAMINATION.
  • Layer 4
    Gate Criteria     Six checklist items: drop threshold, event category match, liquidity check, stabilisation check, 200-day MA duration, and market cap classification. Outputs VALID / REJECT / MONITOR.
  • Layer 5
    Management Credibility     Checks for consecutive earnings misses, repeated guidance cuts, C-suite departures, and restatements. Two or more flags caps Business score at 1 and flags MANAGEMENT CREDIBILITY RISK.
  • Layer 6
    SS Scoring     Four-dimensional score (0–3 each, total 0–12): Clarity, Bounded Downside, Business Strength, Market Reaction. Cap rule: if Business or Clarity ≤1, total capped at 9. Volume and 52W position apply modifiers.
  • Layer 7
    Recovery Catalyst     Identifies the specific upcoming event that would confirm thesis resolution (earnings date, FDA decision, investor day). No catalyst found → Bounded score reduced by 1.
  • Layer 8
    Bear Case     Generates 2–3 specific, factual reasons the trade could fail. Not speculative — based only on available data. Prevents confirmation bias.
  • Layer 9
    Perplexity Flag     Triggers a structured Perplexity Pro research query — copy-paste ready — when the verdict is Watch (7–9) and any signal is ambiguous. Not triggered for Discard or High-quality with clear signals.
  • Layer 10
    Trade Classification     Classifies as BOUNCE (10-day hard exit) or RECOVERY (4-week hard exit + 2-week checkpoint) based on event type and scoring. Calculates stop loss (–8% from current), target (pre-event price), and paper position size.
  • Layer 11
    Output     Produces: (1) human-readable evaluation summary, (2) TSV row formatted for paste into Updates tab, (3) Stock Events alert values to set on entry date.

Evidence, Observations & Current Phase

Experiment phase — data still accumulating. The system is in active paper-trading operation. Meaningful analytics require 60–80 closed cases. Parameter calibration and any retrospective conclusions are deferred to that milestone. The observations below reflect the build and early operational period (April–May 2026) only.

System behaviour observed

The PS scan correctly filters out the majority of alert volume as structural risk or missing data, typically passing 1–3 candidates from a batch of 5–10 email threads. (Observed repeatedly during operational use — not formally benchmarked.)

Evaluation output is reproducible: running the same candidate through the system on different days produces consistent scoring, because the rubric is explicit and AI is constrained by defined criteria rather than open-ended judgment. (Observed — informal comparison only.)

The structured evaluation process reduces the time spent per candidate compared to unstructured manual research, with more consistent output format. (Observed — time estimates are approximate.)

Architectural decisions incorporated

Several design decisions were revised in response to observed failure modes during the build period:

Problem classDesign response
AI response truncation Prompt brevity rules added; output schema constrained; JSON extraction made fault-tolerant with retry logic
Script scope conflicts Configuration centralised to a single object; instruction text separated into a dedicated file to avoid variable collision
Execution time limits Per-run batch size capped; labeling retained on failures so unprocessed items queue to the next run automatically
Data source reliability Price fetching approach redesigned after formula-based methods proved unreliable; fallback logic added

What this section does not yet contain: win rate analysis, scoring accuracy assessment, Gate criterion validation, and parameter calibration recommendations. These require the analytics milestone (≥60 closed cases) and will be added as a separate retrospective section at that point.

Limitations

These limitations are documented explicitly — not to undersell the system, but because honest scoping is part of responsible workflow design.

Input dependency

The system is only as good as its email alert sources. ADVFN, Google Alerts, and Benzinga provide solid coverage of US and UK large-caps, but EU mid-cap and sector-specific events may not surface consistently. Missed events are not detectable — the system cannot flag what it never received.

AI judgment constraints

Gemini (PS extraction) and Claude (Gate+SS evaluation) can misclassify ambiguous cases. The 11-layer rubric constrains this significantly, but it cannot eliminate it. Management credibility flags, sector contamination detection, and catalyst identification all involve judgment calls that may occasionally be wrong.

Paper trading phase

StockSight operates on paper positions only during the experiment phase. The scoring model is not yet calibrated against real outcomes — that requires 60–80 closed cases, which are still accumulating.

No autonomous verification

The PS scan extracts what is written in email alert text. It does not independently verify that a stock actually dropped by the stated percentage on the stated date. Cause verification always uses a separate, independent Google search at evaluation time — not the email source itself.

Apps Script execution limits

Google Apps Script has a 6-minute execution limit per trigger run. For high-volume alert days (>5 threads), processing batches across two daily runs. This means brief delays are possible in extreme cases.
