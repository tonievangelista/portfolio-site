What I Built & Why

Mean reversion on large single-stock price drops is a well-documented strategy (Savor 2012). The practical barrier is not the strategy — it is the pipeline required to act on it reliably. Before StockSight, this meant 30–60 minutes of daily manual inbox scanning, inconsistent evaluation criteria, no structured records, and no feedback loop.

StockSight automates the entire intake-to-verdict pipeline. It monitors financial email alerts around the clock, extracts candidate stocks using Gemini AI, and routes them through an 11-layer structured evaluation framework (Claude AI) before surfacing a scored, documented verdict. All capital decisions remain with the human operator.

Key Design Decisions

AI does judgment inside guardrails — rules do gates

AI is never trusted for binary irreversible decisions. Gemini extracts structured data from noisy text. Claude applies a fixed rubric to produce a scored verdict. Hard gates (fraud, halt, missing data) are deterministic Apps Script rules. Stop loss and target calculations are pure Sheets formulas. This separation prevents AI hallucination from directly affecting any decision point.

Cause verification is always independent

Email alert text is treated as a signal only — never as a source of truth for why a stock dropped. Every PS scan candidate triggers a separate Google Search to verify the cause independently. This is enforced in the Gemini prompt: event and reason fields are always blank in the initial extraction, filled only after search verification.

Experiment phase: all Gate verdicts are informational

For the first 60–80 closed cases, every candidate proceeds to full SS evaluation regardless of Gate outcome. This prevents premature hard-blocking before the system has enough data to validate each criterion. Gate verdicts are logged for future calibration, not used as blockers yet.

Research-informed parameters

Drop thresholds (US 10%, EU 6%), holding periods (BOUNCE 10 days, RECOVERY 4 weeks), RVOL signal classification, and EU vs US volatility adjustments are all grounded in published research — Savor (2012), Morgan Stanley Counterpoint Global (2025), Robeco (2023), and Perplexity Pro deep research (May 2026).

Data-Driven Iteration

Score inversion discovered (18 cases, May 2026): Score 7 Watch cases → 83% win rate, +10.9% average return. Score 9–10 High-quality cases → 16.7% win rate, −10.9% average return. The scoring model was rewarding the wrong signals.

Three targeted changes were made to the evaluation rubric in response, all grounded in research:

Data insightDesign responsePrinciple
HQ earnings/guidance cases losing at high rate Clarity score 3 capped at 2 for Earnings/Guidance/Downgrade events. High-quality verdict blocked for these event types when analyst consensus confirms the repricing. High analytical clarity on an information event = correctly priced repricing, not overreaction. Ambiguity IS the mean reversion signal.
No way to detect analyst consensus alignment New Gate criterion 7: analyst revision coherence check. Scores breadth, direction, magnitude, and language of analyst revisions 0–3. Score 3 reduces Bounded score and flags ANALYST CONSENSUS REPRICING. When analysts revise coherently and directionally, the drop reflects new fundamental information — not a noise-driven overreaction.
Score dimensions never stored — analytics blind spot Four new Tracker columns (AR–AU) capture Clarity, Bounded, Business, and Reaction scores from every evaluation. TSV output extended to include these fields. Can't improve what you can't measure. Dimension-level data enables identifying which scoring criteria actually predict outcomes.

Additional changes from this iteration: Automated exit for experiment phase (EXPERIMENT_AUTO_CLOSE flag) to accelerate closed-case accumulation. Weekly retro price check to distinguish "right thesis, wrong timing" from "wrong thesis" in Loss cases. Sector tagging for sector-level analytics. RECOVERY hard exit corrected from 56 to 28 days per KB v2 research.
