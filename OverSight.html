A Executive Summary

OverSight is an internal decision-support system built for Four Sights Properties to structure, sequence, and constrain the analysis of UK residential property investments. It combines due diligence scoring, HMO feasibility assessment, refurbishment cost modelling, ROI calculation, and offer ceiling logic into a single mandatory workflow — replacing a fragmented set of spreadsheets, manual checks, and disconnected tools.

The system is designed for internal operator use when evaluating deals that involve capital deployment, HMO conversion, or compliance uncertainty. Its primary function is not to generate outputs but to enforce the conditions under which outputs can be trusted: evidence must be present, risk must be surfaced before financial conclusions, and every analytical stage must complete before the next begins.

Why this matters
  • Property investment decisions combine incomplete information, regulatory constraints, market volatility, and irreversible capital deployment. OverSight was designed to reduce decision variance and enforce analytical discipline before offers are made — not to replace human judgment, but to make the preconditions for good judgment consistently visible.
  • OverSight does not approve deals. It determines whether the analytical preconditions for human approval have been met.
B Problem & Baseline
The baseline workflow

Before OverSight, property investment analysis at Four Sights Properties relied on a combination of manual checks across separate tools: Rightmove and Zoopla for listings, SpareRoom for rental demand, council licensing portals for HMO status, spreadsheet models for ROI, and separate interpretation steps for each output. Each component was functional in isolation but disconnected from the others.

The result was a process that was cognitively demanding, inconsistently sequenced, and vulnerable to common failure modes: due diligence steps skipped under time pressure, optimistic rent or refurbishment assumptions carried forward without challenge, and offer decisions reached before compliance or layout feasibility had been assessed.

Non-goals
OverSight explicitly does not:
  • Approve, authorise, or execute investment decisions
  • Provide legal, tax, or regulated financial advice
  • Replace surveyors, solicitors, valuers, architects, or brokers
  • Guarantee ROI, refinance outcomes, or profitability
  • Verify compliance determinations with councils or lenders in real time
  • Operate as a sourcing or deal-finding tool
  • Make autonomous decisions of any kind

These boundaries are not limitations to be resolved in future versions. They reflect deliberate design choices about where AI-assisted analysis is appropriate and where human judgment, professional advice, or physical inspection remain irreplaceable.

D System Design & Workflow
Architecture overview

OverSight is built as a layered analytical pipeline governed by a mandatory sequential workflow called Flow v3. The system is organised into a set of knowledge packs — each responsible for a distinct analytical domain — plus a governing Blueprint layer that defines sequencing rules, kill-switch conditions, authority boundaries, and output constraints.

The pipeline does not allow later stages to run if earlier stages are incomplete or contain unresolved kill-switch risks. This gate logic is the primary mechanism for preventing premature conclusions.

Flow v3 — mandatory workflow sequence
#StageWhat the system does
1Setup & IngestionCollect required inputs (listing URL, floorplan, EPC, comparables, licensing context). Missing critical inputs are flagged immediately and confidence is reduced.
2Due Diligence & Risk ScoringScore 10 DD categories (0–3 each). Surface kill-switch risks. Assign Green / Amber / Red band. Progression blocked if Red.
3HMO Density & CompetitionRequire licensing register and spatial checks before any HMO assumption is made. Flag oversaturated streets.
4Tenant Target FramingPropose viable tenant profiles with confidence labels based on area, demand evidence, and layout potential.
5Value Banding (C3 / As-Is)Establish value ranges from validated sold comparables. No valuation without comparable evidence.
6HMO Feasibility & LayoutsAssess layout options against HMO room-size and compliance rules. Feasibility confidence assigned per option.
7Paths & ExitsDefine viable investment strategies and alternative exit routes. Multi-path modelling surfaces optionality.
8Rents & GDVSet rent assumptions with confidence bands derived from rental comparables and demand indicators.
9Refurb Scope & CostsApply mandatory-first refurb logic. Costs derived from fixed cost matrices only — no free-form estimation.
10ROI ModellingModel multiple price cases using defined financial rules. Stress tests applied. Refinance assumptions labelled.
11MAO & Offer StrategyCalculate Maximum Acceptable Offer using multi-pillar logic. Offer ladders and negotiation guidance produced.
Gate logic

Kill-switch risks detected at Stage 2 halt progression unless a human explicitly acknowledges and overrides. Missing critical inputs at Stage 1 reduce confidence scores and tighten MAO throughout the downstream pipeline — they do not stop analysis entirely, but they remain visible as risk signals in every output.

E Rules, Constraints & Guardrails
Kill-switch conditions

The following conditions halt or block analysis progression regardless of other inputs:

Kill-switch conditionReason for block
Article 4 direction + non-compliant layoutHMO path closed without planning permission
Impossible or non-compliant escape routesFire safety failure — layout rejected
Bedroom size failures (HMO standards)Layout non-viable under licensing rules
Severe structural issues or subsidenceRefurb scope unquantifiable — deal blocked
Severe damp, rot, or contaminationCost uncertainty too high to model reliably
Unworkable kitchen or utility positioningConversion path not viable
Flood zone risk (confirmed)Exit and refinance risk elevated to unacceptable level
Oversaturated HMO streetRental demand and void risk flagged
Confidence downgrade logic

When critical inputs are absent or weak — missing floorplan, outdated comparables, no EPC data — the system does not halt. Instead, it reduces the confidence assigned to affected outputs, widens the uncertainty bands on cost and rent estimates, and tightens the MAO ceiling. Missing data remains explicitly visible throughout the workflow rather than being silently carried forward.

MAO discipline

The Maximum Acceptable Offer is derived from seven independent pillars: sold comparables, active listings, external valuations, yield logic, GDV/HMO value, risk-adjusted view, and finance limit. The output is the minimum safe value across all pillars — not an average, not a target. MAO cannot be calculated unless ROI modelling is complete.

Worked example — Liverpool L13, 3-bed to 5-bed HMO BRRRR

The following shows how OverSight handled a real property evaluation where multiple inputs were missing or uncertain. It illustrates how the system's confidence downgrade and gating logic behaved under realistic conditions.

Real evaluation Liverpool L13 · 3-bed terraced · Asking £138,000 · Target: 5-bed HMO BRRRR
Stage / FactorSystem response
Input stateListing URL and photos provided. No floorplan. EPC D confirmed. Article 4 and licensing status unknown. Visible damp reported.
Stage 1 — IngestionSystem flagged: no floorplan (critical), Article 4 unknown, licensing unknown, damp unassessed. Confidence set to Low for layout; Medium for area.
Stage 2 — DD scoringScore: 11/30 → Amber band. Layout feasibility scored 3/3 (highest risk) due to absent floorplan. Article 4 and licensing each scored 2/3. Analysis allowed to continue — no kill-switch triggered — but all downstream outputs carry reduced confidence.
Stage 3 — HMO densitySystem required licensing register check before HMO assumptions. Operator flagged as pending. HMO density score held at 1/3 pending verification.
Stage 6 — LayoutWithout floorplan, layout feasibility confidence remained Low per Blueprint rules. System generated assumption-based layout scenario (Path A: 5-bed with loft) but labelled it indicative only. Feasibility not confirmed.
Stage 9 — RefurbHeavy-medium conversion scope applied. Cost range £55k–£72k (mid-spec). No free-form figures used — cost matrix applied. Contingency added.
Stage 10 — ROIModelled at asking price (£138k). Estimated cash left in: ~£67k. Annual cashflow: £9k–£13k. ROI on cash left in: 13–19%. Flagged as acceptable but not elite BRRRR recycling.
Stage 11 — MAOMAO tightened by missing floorplan, unknown licensing, damp risk, and absent comparables. Absolute ceiling set at £128k vs asking price of £138k. First offer: £118k.
Final verdictCONDITIONAL CHASE. Area confidence HIGH, rent confidence MED-HIGH, layout confidence LOW, overall deal confidence MEDIUM. Four explicit conditions required before progression: floorplan, damp assessment, Article 4 check, licensing confirmation.

This example illustrates the core design behaviour: the system did not refuse to analyse a property with missing inputs, but it made every gap visible, propagated reduced confidence through all downstream outputs, and tightened the offer ceiling accordingly.

Failure modes addressed during build

The system underwent significant instruction revision between November 2025 and January 2026, driven by observed gaps during repeated use. Changes were not formally logged at the time.

Problem classDesign response
Missing floorplan handlingSystem originally allowed layout assessment without floorplan; revised to require floorplan or reduce feasibility confidence to Low
Comparable age driftOutdated comparables were not flagged; confidence downgrade logic added for comparables outside the accepted threshold
Rent overestimationFree-form rent assumptions allowed; revised to require SpareRoom or comparable evidence before rent figures are accepted
HMO density skippedDensity and licensing register check could be bypassed; gating logic added to block HMO feasibility without this step
ROI without refurb costsROI could be modelled with placeholder refurb figures; mandatory cost matrix reference enforced

No recurring failure modes requiring architectural revision have emerged since Blueprint v2.7.1 was locked in January 2026. The system has been used for ongoing property evaluations without workflow or rule changes over this period.

I Limitations
Stated plainly — these are not caveats to be softened
  • The system cannot physically inspect properties. Structural, damp, and condition assessments depend entirely on what operators observe and report.
  • Layout feasibility assessments are indicative. They are not architectural drawings and do not constitute compliance confirmation.
  • ROI and BRRRR outputs are assumption-dependent. Market shifts, lender policy changes, or execution problems can invalidate modelled figures quickly.
  • Comparables in illiquid or thin markets may be insufficient. The system flags this but cannot resolve it.
  • HMO compliance varies by council interpretation. The system applies general HMO standards; local licensing authority decisions may differ.
  • The system cannot verify council decisions, planning applications, or licensing register entries in real time. Operators must check these externally.
  • AI judgment components (layout interpretation, risk commentary, comparable assessment) can be wrong. They are labelled with confidence levels for this reason.
  • Revision history from the Nov 2025 – Jan 2026 build period was not formally documented.
