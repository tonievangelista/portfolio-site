What it is

OverSight replaces fragmented spreadsheet-based property analysis with a single, sequenced decision-support workflow that combines due diligence, HMO feasibility, refurbishment modelling, ROI calculation, and offer ceiling logic.

The system does not produce outputs and then ask humans to interpret them. It enforces the conditions under which outputs can be trusted — evidence first, risk visible, sequencing mandatory. Every analytical stage must complete before the next begins.

It was built to solve a specific operational problem: investment decisions were being made with incomplete due diligence, inconsistent assumptions, and no enforced analytical order. OverSight makes it structurally difficult to reach an offer decision before the groundwork has been done.

Key capabilities
🔒
Kill-switch enforcement
8 hard-stop conditions that halt analysis unconditionally — Article 4, structural failure, fire safety, flood risk. No AI reasoning involved.
📉
Confidence downgrade logic
Missing data doesn't stop the analysis — it reduces confidence, widens uncertainty bands, and tightens the offer ceiling throughout the pipeline.
🧮
Deterministic financial engine
SDLT, ROI, BRRRR, and refinance calculations use fixed tiered formulas. Validated against partners' Excel model across three evaluated deals.
⚖️
Multi-pillar MAO
Maximum Acceptable Offer derived from 7 independent pillars. Output is the minimum safe value across all — not an average, not a target.
🔀
Deal comparison engine
Normalises and ranks multiple properties across 12 criteria. Separates investment performance from defensive hold quality.
👤
Human-in-the-loop design
Explicit human gates at every decision point. Kill-switch overrides require human acknowledgement. Final decisions never owned by the system.
Scope & boundaries
The system does
  • Enforce a mandatory 11-stage analysis sequence
  • Score due diligence risk across 10 categories
  • Assess HMO layout feasibility with confidence labels
  • Calculate ROI, BRRRR, and SDLT using fixed formulas
  • Produce a conservative, multi-pillar offer ceiling
  • Flag and propagate missing data as risk
  • Compare and rank multiple properties consistently
The system does not
  • Approve, authorise, or execute investment decisions
  • Provide legal, tax, or regulated financial advice
  • Replace surveyors, solicitors, or valuers
  • Guarantee ROI, refinance outcomes, or profitability
  • Verify licensing or planning decisions in real time
  • Make autonomous decisions of any kind
